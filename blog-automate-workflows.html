The five workflows every service business should automate

7 min read

Most service businesses are not short on work, they're short on time. The hours that disappear into repetitive admin are hours that don't go toward serving customers or growing the business. Here are the five workflows we see costing the most time, and what automating each one actually looks like.

1. Lead follow-up

A lead who doesn't hear back within a few hours is a lead who has probably already called someone else. Automated follow-up sequences make sure every enquiry gets a timely response, a reminder if they go quiet, and a nudge to book, without anyone having to remember to send it.

2. Appointment reminders

No-shows cost real money, and most of them come down to people simply forgetting. An automated reminder by text or WhatsApp the day before and the morning of an appointment reduces no-shows without anyone on your team picking up the phone.

3. Invoicing and collections

Chasing unpaid invoices is one of the least enjoyable parts of running a service business, and it's also one of the easiest to automate. Invoices can go out automatically when a job is marked complete, with reminders sent on a schedule until payment lands.

4. Client onboarding

New clients usually need the same set of information: intake forms, welcome details, what to expect next. Automating onboarding means every new client gets a consistent, professional experience from day one, without your team manually sending the same emails over and over.

5. Review and reputation requests

Happy customers don't always think to leave a review unless you ask at the right moment. An automated request sent shortly after a job is completed, while the experience is still fresh, consistently brings in more reviews than asking manually ever does.

Where to start

You don't need to automate everything at once. Most of our clients start with whichever workflow is costing them the most time right now, see the hours come back, and expand from there. A free audit is the fastest way to find out which one that is for your business.
